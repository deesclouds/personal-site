About

During my stay at the Fashion Institute of Technology, I majored in Fine Arts and Art History. Soon after FIT, I began working as an art assistant for local artists in New York City. Following that, I transferred to Pace University to major in photography with minors in non-profit studies and queer studies. After Pace, I worked as an intern for local photographers before showing my work at the International Center of Photography, where I eventually became a teaching assistant for aspiring photographers.


The pandemic struck when I was working as a teaching assistant, causing the school to close. I moved to Long Beach, California, to seek a job in programming and fell in love with the opportunity to express my artistic side while also learning about software and marketing. To be able to share my thoughts with the rest of the world, I studied software engineering and tried to build online experiences similar to those at ICP. While museum displays are now available online, I considered how I might make my skills more accessible. I've spent the last year developing my software engineering and digital marketing skills, with the goal of using code to create attractive experiences for the broader public.
